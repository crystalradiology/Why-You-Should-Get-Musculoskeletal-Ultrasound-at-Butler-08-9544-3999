What Is a Musculoskeletal Ultrasound?
A musculoskeletal ultrasound is a non-invasive imaging test that uses high-frequency sound waves to produce detailed images of muscles, tendons, ligaments, joints, and soft tissues.
Unlike X-rays, which mainly show bones, ultrasound allows doctors to examine soft tissues in real time.
During the scan:
•	A small amount of gel is applied to the skin
•	A handheld device called a transducer is moved over the affected area
•	Sound waves create live images on a monitor
This allows doctors to observe movement within joints and tissues while identifying inflammation, tears, or other abnormalities.

Uses of Musculoskeletal Ultrasound
Musculoskeletal ultrasound is widely used in diagnosing many conditions affecting the muscles, joints, and connective tissues.
Below are some of the most common uses of this imaging test.
1. Detecting Tendon Abnormalities
Tendons connect muscles to bones and are frequently affected by injury or overuse.
•	Ultrasound can detect conditions such as:
•	Tendonitis (inflammation of tendons)
•	Tendon tears
•	Rotator cuff injuries
•	Tendon degeneration
•	Tendon spasms
These injuries are common in athletes, manual workers, and individuals performing repetitive tasks.
Doctors often recommend that patients get a musculoskeletal ultrasound at Butler to confirm tendon injuries and guide treatment.
2. Identifying Muscle Injuries
Muscle injuries are among the most common causes of pain and mobility problems.
Musculoskeletal ultrasound helps detect:
•	Muscle tears
•	Muscle strains
•	Internal bleeding or fluid collection
•	Muscle masses or abnormalities
Because ultrasound produces real-time images, doctors can observe muscle movement during the scan.
3. Diagnosing Joint Abnormalities
Joint pain can result from inflammation, injury, or degenerative conditions such as arthritis.
Musculoskeletal ultrasound helps identify:
•	Joint swelling
•	Fluid buildup (effusion)
•	Ligament damage
•	Joint inflammation
Patients experiencing persistent joint pain are often advised to Get Musculoskeletal Ultrasound at Butler for a detailed assessment.
4. Detecting Cartilage Lesions
Cartilage plays an important role in allowing smooth joint movement.
Cartilage damage may cause:
•	Pain during movement
•	Reduced mobility
•	Joint stiffness
Ultrasound imaging can detect cartilage lesions and early signs of joint damage.
5. Detecting Hernias
Musculoskeletal ultrasound can also identify hernias, which occur when internal tissues push through weakened muscle walls.
Common types detected include:
•	Inguinal hernias
•	Abdominal wall hernias
•	Soft tissue hernias
Ultrasound provides a clear view of the affected area, helping doctors confirm the diagnosis.
